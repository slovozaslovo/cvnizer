# The executive CV register — what right sounds like

Distilled from published industry guidance on senior/executive CV writing (sources in
README). This is the wording target cvnizer converges toward. It covers HOW things are
said, not what is claimed — fact-checking is out of scope.

## Voice: implied first person ("resume speak")

- **No pronouns.** Not "I led...", not "She leads...", not "John manages...". The
  standard is the pronoun dropped and the verb leading: "Led a 15-person department."
- Pronoun discipline gets stricter with seniority. At director level and above,
  first person reads junior; narrated third person reads pompous.
- **Tense:** past tense for past roles, present (base form) for the current role.
  Never mix conjugated narration into verb-led text: "Sets direction" mixed with
  "Built a team" breaks the register — use "Set direction".
- Profile/summary openers are noun phrases: "[Title] with N years in X...", then
  verb-led sentences.

## Register: direct, factual, commercial

- **Not conversational.** No asides ("which was a nice bonus", "and it actually worked"),
  no self-narration ("lately I have been focusing on"), no charm, no storytelling.
- **Not inflated.** No fluff adjectives (dynamic, passionate, results-driven,
  visionary) — they claim without evidence. State the outcome; let it carry the weight.
- Plain business vocabulary over craft jargon or AI-tell words (leverage, empower,
  synergy, cutting-edge).
- Confident and unhedged: no "helped with", "was involved in", "might", "somewhat"
  when the writer owned the work.

## Rhythm

- Vary sentence length. Uniform 20-word sentences read machine-made.
- Break forced triads ("fast, reliable, and scalable") unless all three earn their place.
- No em or en dashes anywhere — restructure with a period, comma, colon, or semicolon.

## Density

- Every word earns its place. Cut filler ("in order to", "responsible for the
  management of", "a wide variety of").
- Brevity signals seniority: a 15+ year career summarized in 4-5 tight sentences reads
  more senior than one that runs long.

## What cvnizer does NOT judge

Truthfulness, metric selection, section structure, and seniority of the underlying
claims are content decisions, not wording. Pair cvnizer with your own fact-check and
CV-structure review. If a sentence is a lie, cvnizer will only make it a well-worded lie.
