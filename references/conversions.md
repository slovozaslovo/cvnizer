# Conversion pairs — applicant language → executive CV language

Before→after pairs, grouped by failure type. These are the loop's few-shot examples:
when rewriting, find the pair whose BEFORE matches the sentence's failure and convert
the same way. Facts always come from the source under review — copy the *move*, never
the content. (All pairs below are illustrative composites, not any real person's CV.)

## Self-narration → verb-led implied first person

- BEFORE: "I am responsible for leading the platform team and I make sure everything we release meets the bar."
- AFTER: "Lead the platform team and own release quality end to end."

- BEFORE: "Recently I have been working a lot with analytics tooling, dashboards and automation."
- AFTER: "Introduced analytics tooling, dashboard standards, and reporting automation."

- BEFORE: "My first management role: built and led a support team..."
- AFTER: "First management role: built and led a support team of 6..."

## Product/feature narration → what the leader delivered

- BEFORE: "One of the apps we made lets clinic staff book equipment from a tablet, it shows availability in real time so nobody has to call around anymore. The other one sends field technicians their daily jobs with all the paperwork handled right in the app."
- AFTER: "Built two scheduling apps from scratch: equipment booking time fell 70%; technicians start their routes 40 minutes earlier each day."
- THE MOVE: the subject returns to the builder; the app's UX detail becomes the operation's measured outcome; outcomes attach to the products (shared wins stay structurally attributed).

## IC/artifact framing → system-level object (altitude)

- BEFORE: "Wrote a dashboard generator and a couple of monitoring scripts that check our data pipelines overnight."
- AFTER: "Established overnight pipeline monitoring: automated checks and generated dashboards surface data issues before business hours."
- THE MOVE: the practice (already in the sentence) becomes the object; the scripts demote to mechanism. Nothing invented.

- BEFORE: "Just as a side project really, I built a small tool that made our data checks about 4x faster."
- AFTER: "Accelerated data quality checks 4x by introducing automated validation into the ingestion flow."
- THE MOVE: casual hedges deleted ("just", "really", "small"); the tool demotes to mechanism; the measured outcome leads.

## Ops-KPI vocabulary → commercial vocabulary (same fact)

- BEFORE: "tickets closed per analyst went from about 30 to 90 a week"
- AFTER: "support throughput tripled"
- THE MOVE: small absolutes read shop-floor; the ratio at function level reads senior. The raw numbers stay in the fact record / interview.

- BEFORE: "we now do reviews after every major incident, which I made a standard thing"
- AFTER: "standardized post-incident reviews across the team"
- THE MOVE: rambling practice description → single strong verb owning the practice.

## Negative disclaimer → positive claim standing alone

- BEFORE: "Set the marketing analytics roadmap and priorities, although I am not a data scientist myself."
- AFTER: "Set the marketing analytics roadmap and priorities across three regional teams."
- THE MOVE: delete the boundary clause entirely; spend the words on scope instead (scope facts must exist in the source).

## Reduction verb ↔ positive claim (both legitimate; claim-then-proof keeps the number honest)

- BEFORE: "Redesigned the customer onboarding flow. Failed activations dropped 60% within a quarter."
- AFTER: "Cut failed activations 60% in one quarter by redesigning the customer onboarding flow."
- ALTERNATE (positive frame): "Raised onboarding reliability: failed activations fell 60%..." — the number must stay attached to what was measured; "improved onboarding 60%" would be fabrication.

## Tool logos → capability words

- BEFORE: "Reduced monthly report generation from six hours to 20 minutes after our ETL rewrite (Airflow, dbt)."
- AFTER: "Accelerated reporting: generation time down from six hours to 20 minutes through an ETL pipeline rewrite."

- BEFORE: "Built dashboards for the sales team (Tableau, Looker) and got everyone off the old spreadsheets."
- AFTER: "Built self-serve analytics capability for the sales team, retiring spreadsheet reporting."

## Apologetic size words → unsized ownership

- BEFORE: "A tiny group of us put together an internal component library that other teams now reuse in their projects."
- AFTER: "Led the group that shipped an internal component library, now reused across project teams."
- THE MOVE: unsized is not inflated, just silent; altitude comes from owning the outcome.

## Trailing -ing commentary → stop at the content

- BEFORE: "...trained the finance department on the new planning system, streamlining and improving how they budget and forecast."
- AFTER: "...trained the finance department on the new planning system."
